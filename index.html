Balance Your Context

Low-context vs. high-context cultures

Low-context cultures assume readers are bringing little context to their reading. These cultures tend to provide a lot of detail in communications. High-context cultures assume the reader already has sufficient context and prior knowledge, making their communications much more concise.

When communicating with different cultures, balancing context is key.

Understanding Your Readers

Establishing credibility is highly dependent on how well you understand your readers. Writing documentation is all about anticipating how and why people will want to use your software. The better you can anticipate the needs of your readers, the more thorough and accessible your documentation will be.

When working on a project for an extended amount of time, you become an expert on its inner-workings. The more familiar you are with software, the more difficult it becomes to document the simpler use-cases. Writing your documentation as you build will help you maintain your hindsight, and prevent you from neglecting a portion of your audience.
